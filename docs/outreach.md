# Cold outreach playbook — getting referral/affiliate deals

Goal: get a referral code, affiliate link, or exclusive student discount from
each AI company. Send from a real personal email (not a generic one), keep it
short, and make the ask tiny.

## Before you email anyone

1. **Check if they already have an affiliate program.** Search
   "`<company> affiliate program`" and check their site footer. If they do,
   just apply — no cold email needed. (Many run on Impact, PartnerStack, or
   Rewardful.)
2. **Know your numbers.** Have real traffic/audience stats ready. If you don't
   have them yet, lead with the schools instead — "built by students at
   Northwestern, Stanford, UCLA..." is your credibility until traffic is.
3. **Find the right person.** Partnerships/growth manager on LinkedIn, or
   partnerships@/marketing@ addresses. Founders at small startups.

## The template

> **Subject: Student distribution for [Product] — Northwestern/Stanford student project**
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm [your name], a student at [school]. With friends at Northwestern,
> Stanford, UCLA, Princeton, and a few other schools, we run StudentStack —
> a directory of AI tools with student deals ([link]).
>
> Students constantly ask us about [Product] — it's one of the most viewed
> tools on our site. We'd love to send you those users officially:
>
> 1. A referral/affiliate link so we can track what we send you, and/or
> 2. A student-only offer we can feature (even a small one — students
>    convert when there's a student price)
>
> We only list tools we actually use, and every deal links straight to your
> own checkout — we never resell or discount without permission.
>
> Worth a quick chat? Happy to share our traffic numbers.
>
> [Name] · [school] · [site link]

## Rules of thumb

- **One follow-up after ~5 days**, then stop. ("Bumping this in case it got
  buried — worth a look?")
- **Track everything** in a sheet: company, contact, date sent, reply, terms.
- **Ask for 20–30% of first payment** (or whatever their standard program
  pays) when terms come up — recurring commission if you can get it.
- **Read the agreement before signing.** Some programs ban "deal/coupon
  sites" — ask about it upfront. If you're under 18, a parent may need to
  sign and set up the payout account.
- **Never promise placement you can't deliver** (traffic numbers, rankings).
  The site's credibility is the whole product.

## Who to hit first

Smaller companies say yes faster and give better terms. From the current
catalog, rough order: Gamma, Otter, Speechify, Quizlet, Wolfram — then the
giants (Notion, Grammarly, Canva have established programs; just apply).
Perplexity/OpenAI/GitHub: don't cold-email; use their official programs if
any exist.
